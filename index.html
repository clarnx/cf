FAQ (Frequently Asked Questions)

How much do you charge for a website?

How much does a car cost? It depends. What’s it for? Which features are desired? By when do you need it? In other words, there’s no preset pricing, as it’s dependent on the project’s needs and available resources.

How long will it take to complete my website?

Similarly to the price, this also depends on your project’s requirements and resources. As the saying goes, “Fast, good, or cheap: Pick two.”

Why should I work with you instead of SquareSpace, Wix, etc.?

While you would be paying rock-bottom prices for services like that, you may also receive rock-bottom results. If a slow, bloated, limited web experience is ideal for your business, using those services may be a sufficient option, just not one we’d recommend. Frugality is great, but you usually get what you pay for.

What do you mean by "owning my website"? Isn't that to be expected?

Not necessarily. Other designers and developers may prefer to host your site on their personal accounts or servers, either to make it easier for you or to have more control of their code. We want you to own your website. Once you do the initial setup (which we can help you with), you’ll have 100% ownership of your website’s domain and hosting.
However, if you’re someone who absolutely does not want to deal with hosting themselves, let us know. You can be accommodated.

Will there be any maintenance?

Your website will be created in such a way that maintenance will be very minimal, if at all necessary. Should something unforeseen pop up within a month after launch, let us know so we can take a look. Any substantial changes desired after that time period will require us to plan a new project. If you want help to be available long after that initial month, you can set up a maintenance plan with us.

What if I'm unhappy with the design? Will there be any revisions?

Yes, you will receive two rounds of revisions. Beyond that, there would be additional charges.

How does payment work?

There’s a 50% deposit due at the very start, which accomplishes two things:
1) You only pay half upfront, so your initial financial burden is lowered.
2) It provides us the resources needed to give your project the best possible start.
Once both rounds of revisions are complete, you will be charged the remaining 50% right before your website goes live.

What is the refund policy?

The initial deposit is non-refundable. If you were to refund a TV, you’d get your money back and the company can then resell that TV to someone else. If you were to refund your website, we cannot resell it to anyone else because it was made specifically for your needs and your taste. The cost for those expended resources must be covered. As such, there are no refunds.
